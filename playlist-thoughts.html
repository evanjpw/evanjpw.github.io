Playlist Thoughts

I didn't want to spam your phone with a wall of text, and since I didn't know your personal email, and it felt weird (I don't know why) to send it to your work email, I just made a page.

Before I say anything, I want to say thank you for the thoughtful & wonderful playlist. I really had fun listening to it. And I can see the care that you put into it, and that makes it feel special.

I am charmed by your eclectic taste in music, and all of the richness that was captured in your choices. Every time there was a new track, I couldn't wait to see what treat would be next for me, and every one made me very happy. I never knew what to expect, but it was always a delight.

One thing that struck me on the second run through is that you like songs with powerful images, and so do I. I liked that quite a bit.

And I feel like you put a bunch of effort into the order, and that really worked for me. Thank you.

I clearly have to up my game! I am working on my next playlist for you. It is amazing getting to know you this way, and I hope that you feel as I do.

That said, here are my thoughts about the songs:

  1. Watermelon Man - Of course I love this song, but before you played it for me I had forgotten about it. It has one of those grooves that gets into your bones, and I am so appreciative that you reintroduced me to my old friend.

  2. Daqat - This was such a fantastic surprise. It was an infectious tune, and it just sounds so awesome. The sound of a language gives so much color to a song, and this was nothing I was expecting but the Arabic lyrics felt perfect. I know that I will listen to this one a lot. (Also, the whistling captured my heart.)

  3. Cocaine Jesus - I really like this one. I like that it has so many moods in one song, and I like that it gets stuck in my head and then evolves and gets stuck again. And I like the pictures it paints.

  4. Back Pocket - This feels like Prince in places and then it changes and keeps changing and then it's on the playground and then clarinets and it is a wonderful ride and I keep wanting to ride it again. This makes me smile. YouTube keeps trying to sell me Vulfpeck, and I have not been biting. Thank you for convincing me!

  5. Talk to the Moon - Thank you so much for introducing me to Ripe. Everything I have heard makes me want to dance and I love his voice and I love the horns (and, as a bass player, I dig the bass lines). This makes me happy.

  6. My Type - This reminds me of a few songs that I really like, and I really enjoy the way it feels, musically and lyrically.

  7. My Love - This one is very tasty. It is sweet and it delights me and it makes me float and it carries that playful, beautiful feeling that comes at the start of a relationship with it and I am enraptured by it. This may be my favorite from the list, but don't hold me to that.

  8. The Mother We Share - I feel very energized by this one, and I like the lyrics, and the catchiness. It is one that I feel like I need to listen to many more times.

  9. Muddy Waters - Slow and compelling. And her voice is wonderful. It sinks into my soul. I really enjoyed this and I have to listen to more from LP.

  10. Breezeblocks - I mentioned the sound of the language before, and this one is in English, but the sound of the English is captivating and fits the song like a glove. I like the imagery and it's just fun.

  11. Excuses - This feels like many songs that I like. I kept backing it up and listening to this song again. I probably replayed it more than any of these songs.

  12. Lost - So, I had considered a remix of "Thinkin Bout You" that I like for your playlist, but I had never heard this song and I really, really like it. It's got a lot going on, and I enjoy that.

  13. 40 Day Dream - I like the mix of feelings from this one. It has a bar band feel, but then is so much smarter and more interesting. I plan to listen to more songs from them.

  14. Ride - There is a theme here of songs that, I don't know how to put it, defy expectations, or maybe categorization. This one grew on me after the first listening. It has worked its way into my heart.

  15. Ready - What finish! I used the word compelling before, but this one turns compelling up to eleven. It has a visceral appeal, and it is totally ecstatic music. I really enjoyed this one and I listened to it several times.

Favorites are difficult but, if I have to: Back Pocket, My Love, Ready, Daqat, My Type

Thank you again for this playlist. I really am so grateful for it and I love that we are sharing these!
